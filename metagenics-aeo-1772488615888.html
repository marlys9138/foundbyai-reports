Your 6 AI Visibility Deliverables
Layer 1 · Upload to your website
Answer Hub Page
The most high-impact page you can add to your website. Structured as a buying guide — the kind of neutral, authoritative content that AI models like ChatGPT and Perplexity learn to cite when someone asks about your category.
WHAT TO DO WITH THIS
1
Save the file as answer-hub.html (or paste the HTML into a new page in your CMS).
2
Publish it at a clean URL — ideally something like yoursite.com/best-professional-grade-nutritional-supplements-for-gut-health-and-immune-support or /guide.
3
Link to it from your homepage or navigation so search engines and AI crawlers can find it easily.
4
Submit the URL to Google Search Console — go to URL Inspection → paste the URL → Request Indexing.
5
Keep it public — never put this behind a login or paywall. It only works if AI can read it freely.
Layer 3 · Requires developer
Schema Markup & Brand JSON
The machine-readable layer — invisible to visitors but essential for AI. Tells search engines and AI crawlers exactly how to classify your brand, products, and FAQs.
WHAT TO DO WITH THIS
1
Copy the JSON-LD block and give it to your developer to paste inside the <head> tag of your homepage.
2
For the brand-facts.json file — ask your developer to upload it to /.well-known/brand-facts.json on your server.
3
Test your schema after it's live at validator.schema.org — paste your homepage URL and check for errors.
4
No developer? Most platforms (Shopify, Squarespace, Webflow) have a "custom code" section in settings where you can paste JSON-LD directly.
Layer 4 · Action checklist
Google Merchant Center Checklist
ChatGPT now surfaces shopping results. If your product listings aren't optimised, you're invisible to AI-driven purchase intent.
WHAT TO DO WITH THIS
1
Open Google Merchant Center — go to Products → All Products and review your current feed health.
2
